## Core Strategy Frameworks

### 1. Porter's Five Forces

**Framework Overview:**
Analyzes industry attractiveness and competitive intensity

**The Five Forces:**

1. **Threat of New Entrants**
   - Barriers to entry (capital, regulation, technology)
   - Economies of scale
   - Brand loyalty and switching costs
   - Access to distribution channels

2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers**
   - Supplier concentration
   - Switching costs
   - Uniqueness of inputs
   - Forward integration potential

3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers**
   - Buyer concentration
   - Price sensitivity
   - Product differentiation
   - Backward integration potential

4. **Threat of Substitutes**
   - Alternative solutions
   - Price-performance trade-offs
   - Switching costs
   - Buyer propensity to substitute

5. **Competitive Rivalry**
   - Number and diversity of competitors
   - Industry growth rate
   - Fixed costs and capacity
   - Exit barriers

### 2. Value Chain Analysis

**Framework Overview:**
Breaks down a company's activities to identify sources of competitive advantage

**Primary Activities:**
- Inbound Logistics
- Operations
- Outbound Logistics
- Marketing & Sales
- Service

**Support Activities:**
- Firm Infrastructure
- Human Resource Management
- Technology Development
- Procurement

**Application Process:**

1. Map all activities in the value chain
2. Identify cost drivers for each activity
3. Identify sources of differentiation
4. Analyze linkages between activities
5. Determine where competitive advantage exists

### 3. SWOT Analysis

**Framework Overview:**
Strategic planning tool assessing Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats

**Internal (Current State):**
- **Strengths**: What you do well
- **Weaknesses**: What you lack or do poorly

**External (Future Potential):**
- **Opportunities**: External factors you can exploit
- **Threats**: External factors that could harm you

### 4. Blue Ocean Strategy

**Framework Overview:**
Create uncontested market space by making competition irrelevant

**Key Concepts:**
- **Red Ocean**: Existing market space, fierce competition
- **Blue Ocean**: Untapped market space, new demand creation

**Four Actions Framework:**

1. **Eliminate**: Which factors the industry takes for granted should be eliminated?
2. **Reduce**: Which factors should be reduced well below industry standard?
3. **Raise**: Which factors should be raised well above industry standard?
4. **Create**: Which factors should be created that the industry has never offered?

**Application Template:**

```
- [Factor 2]: [Why create]

[How this creates a new market space]
```

### 5. Scenario Planning

**Framework Overview:**
Prepare for multiple possible futures by developing plausible scenarios

**Process:**
1. Identify focal issue or decision
2. Identify key driving forces
3. Rank by importance and uncertainty
4. Select scenario logics (2x2 matrix)
5. Flesh out scenarios
6. Identify implications and strategic options

**Scenario Template:**

### 6. Capital Allocation Framework

**Framework Overview:**
Prioritize where to invest limited resources for maximum return

**Options for Capital:**
1. Reinvest in existing business (organic growth)
2. Acquisitions (inorganic growth)
3. Return to shareholders (dividends, buybacks)
4. Pay down debt
5. Hold as cash/reserves

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Expected ROI (Return on Investment)
- Risk profile
- Strategic fit
- Time horizon
- Optionality

### 7. Business Model Canvas

**9 Building Blocks:**

1. **Customer Segments**: Who are we serving?
2. **Value Propositions**: What problem do we solve?
3. **Channels**: How do we deliver value?
4. **Customer Relationships**: How do we interact?
5. **Revenue Streams**: How do we make money?
6. **Key Resources**: What assets do we need?
7. **Key Activities**: What do we do?
8. **Key Partnerships**: Who helps us?
9. **Cost Structure**: What are our costs?

### 8. Ansoff Growth Matrix

**2x2 Matrix of Growth Strategies:**

|  | Existing Products | New Products |
|---|---|---|
| **Existing Markets** | Market Penetration | Product Development |
| **New Markets** | Market Development | Diversification |

**When to Use Each:**
- **Market Penetration**: Increase share in current market
- **Market Development**: Expand to new geographies or segments
- **Product Development**: Create new products for current customers
- **Diversification**: New products in new markets (highest risk)

## Case Study Approach

### Case Study Structure

**1. Situation Analysis (20%)**
- Understand the context
- Identify the problem
- Gather key facts

**2. Problem Identification (15%)**
- Define the core issue
- Distinguish symptoms from root causes
- Frame the question

**3. Framework Selection (10%)**
- Choose appropriate analytical tool
- May use multiple frameworks

**4. Analysis (35%)**
- Apply frameworks
- Develop insights
- Consider alternatives

**5. Recommendation (20%)**
- Propose solution
- Justify with analysis
- Address implementation
