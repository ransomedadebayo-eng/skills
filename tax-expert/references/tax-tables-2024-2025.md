## 2. Capital Gains Tax Rates

### Long-Term Capital Gains (assets held >1 year)

**2024 Rates by Ordinary Income:**

**Single Filers:**
| Taxable Income | LTCG Rate |
|----------------|-----------|
| $0 - $47,025 | 0% |
| $47,026 - $518,900 | 15% |
| $518,901+ | 20% |

**Married Filing Jointly:**
| Taxable Income | LTCG Rate |
|----------------|-----------|
| $0 - $94,050 | 0% |
| $94,051 - $583,750 | 15% |
| $583,751+ | 20% |

**Additional:** 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) applies when MAGI exceeds $200K (single) or $250K (MFJ)

### Short-Term Capital Gains (assets held ≤1 year)
Taxed as ordinary income at regular tax brackets

### Qualified Dividends
Same rates as long-term capital gains (0%, 15%, or 20%)

### Collectibles & Section 1250 Property
- Collectibles: Max 28% rate
- Depreciation recapture (real estate): 25% rate

## 3. Retirement Contribution Limits

### 2024 Limits

**401(k), 403(b), 457 Plans:**
- Employee contribution: $23,000
- Catch-up (age 50+): +$7,500
- Total limit (employee + employer): $69,000

**IRA (Traditional & Roth):**
- Contribution limit: $7,000
- Catch-up (age 50+): +$1,000
- Total with catch-up: $8,000

**SEP-IRA:**
- Up to 25% of compensation (20% of net SE income)
- Maximum contribution: $69,000

**SIMPLE IRA:**
- Employee contribution: $16,000
- Catch-up (age 50+): +$3,500
- Employer match: 3% or 2% non-elective

**Solo 401(k):**
- Employee deferral: $23,000
- Employer contribution: 25% of compensation (20% of net SE income)
- Total maximum: $69,000
- Catch-up (age 50+): +$7,500 = $76,500 total

**HSA (Health Savings Account):**
- Individual: $4,150
- Family: $8,300
- Catch-up (age 55+): +$1,000

### IRA Deduction Phase-outs (2024)

**Traditional IRA (if covered by workplace plan):**
- Single/HoH: $77,000 - $87,000
- MFJ: $123,000 - $143,000
- MFS: $0 - $10,000

**Roth IRA Contribution Phase-outs:**
- Single/HoH: $146,000 - $161,000
- MFJ: $230,000 - $240,000
- MFS: $0 - $10,000

## 5. Social Security & Medicare Taxes (FICA)

### Self-Employment Tax (2024)
- Social Security: 12.4% on first $168,600 of net SE income
- Medicare: 2.9% on all net SE income
- Additional Medicare: +0.9% on income over $200K (single) / $250K (MFJ)
- **Total SE tax rate:** 15.3% (on income up to SS wage base)

### W-2 Employment Tax
- Employee portion: 7.65% (6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare)
- Employer portion: 7.65%
- Additional Medicare on employee: +0.9% over thresholds

### Social Security Wage Base
- 2024: $168,600
- 2025: TBD (typically increases ~3% annually)

## 6. Important Thresholds & Phase-outs

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) - 3.8%
Applies to investment income when MAGI exceeds:
- Single/HoH: $200,000
- MFJ: $250,000
- MFS: $125,000

### Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) Exemption (2024)
**Exemption amounts:**
- Single: $85,700 (phase-out starts at $609,350)
- MFJ: $133,300 (phase-out starts at $1,218,700)
- MFS: $66,650 (phase-out starts at $609,350)

**AMT rates:**
- 26% on first $220,700 (MFJ) / $110,350 (others)
- 28% above that threshold

### Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction
- Up to 20% deduction on qualified business income
- Phase-out for specified service trades/businesses (SSTB):
  - Single: $191,950 - $241,950
  - MFJ: $383,900 - $483,900

### Premium Tax Credit (ACA)
- Available for household income 100%-400% of federal poverty level
- Enhanced subsidies may extend beyond 400% through 2025

### Estate & Gift Tax (2024)
- Lifetime exemption: $13,610,000 per person
- Annual gift exclusion: $18,000 per recipient
- Portability: Surviving spouse can use deceased spouse's unused exemption

## 7. Business Tax Information

### Section 179 Expensing (2024)
- Maximum deduction: $1,220,000
- Phase-out threshold: $3,050,000
- Qualifying property: Equipment, vehicles, software, certain improvements

### Bonus Depreciation
- 2024: 60%
- 2025: 40%
- 2026: 20%
- 2027 and beyond: 0%

### Qualified Improvement Property (QIP)
- 15-year recovery period
- Eligible for bonus depreciation

### Business Meal Deduction
- Business meals: 50% deductible
- Employee meals (convenience of employer): 50%
- Company parties/picnics: 100%

### Standard Mileage Rates (2024)
- Business: 67¢ per mile
- Medical/moving: 21¢ per mile
- Charity: 14¢ per mile

### Home Office
- Simplified method: $5/sq ft (max 300 sq ft = $1,500)
- Regular method: Actual expenses × business use percentage

### Augusta Rule (Section 280A)
- Rent home to business up to 14 days/year
- Income tax-free to homeowner
- Business can deduct as expense

## 9. Key Tax Planning Dates

### Quarterly Estimated Tax Deadlines
- Q1 (Jan 1 - Mar 31): Due April 15
- Q2 (Apr 1 - May 31): Due June 15
- Q3 (Jun 1 - Aug 31): Due September 15
- Q4 (Sep 1 - Dec 31): Due January 15 (following year)

### Annual Deadlines
- Individual returns (1040): April 15
- Extension deadline: October 15
- Partnership/S-Corp returns: March 15
- C-Corp returns: April 15
- IRA contributions (prior year): April 15
- HSA contributions (prior year): April 15

### Tax Planning Deadlines
- Most tax moves: December 31
- 529 contributions (for state deduction): Varies by state, often December 31
- Charity contributions: December 31 (postmark date)
- Roth conversions: December 31
- Section 179 equipment purchases: December 31

## 10. Safe Harbor Rules for Estimated Taxes

Avoid underpayment penalty if you pay:
- 90% of current year tax liability, OR
- 100% of prior year tax liability (110% if AGI >$150K)

**Exception:** Can skip Q4 payment if file return and pay in full by January 31
