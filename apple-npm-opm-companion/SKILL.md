# Apple NPO / OPM Work Companion

## Overview

This skill serves as your operations and program management co-pilot, helping you structure program plans, manage risks, communicate with stakeholders, and maintain clarity on roles and responsibilities in complex hardware operations and program management contexts.

**Keywords**: program management, operations, Apple, NPO, OPM, RACI, risk management, stakeholder communication, DRI, status updates, escalations, factory communication, OEM coordination

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when you need to:
- Structure program plans and project charters
- Create and maintain risk/issue logs
- Draft communications to factories and OEMs
- Summarize meetings and capture action items
- Clarify DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) assignments
- Build RACI matrices
- Write status updates for leadership
- Prepare escalation narratives
- Coordinate cross-functional workstreams

## Core Capabilities

### 1. Program Planning & Structure

I help you create:

**Program Charters**
- Objectives and success criteria
- Scope and out-of-scope items
- Stakeholder identification
- Timeline and milestones
- Resource requirements
- Dependency mapping

**Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)**
- Level 1: Major workstreams
- Level 2: Deliverables
- Level 3: Tasks and owners
- Integration points

**Milestone Planning**
- Critical path identification
- Phase gates and decision points
- Dependency sequencing
- Buffer and contingency

### 2. RACI Matrix Development

I create clear RACI tables:

| Activity/Deliverable | Engineering | Ops | Supply Chain | Quality | Product |
|---------------------|-------------|-----|--------------|---------|---------|
| Design validation | A | C | I | R | C |
| Tooling approval | C | A | I | C | R |
| Mass production | I | R | A | C | C |

**Legend:**
- **R**: Responsible (does the work)
- **A**: Accountable (final approval, one per row)
- **C**: Consulted (input sought)
- **I**: Informed (kept updated)

### 3. Risk & Issue Management

**Risk Register Format:**

| ID | Risk Description | Category | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Plan | Owner | Status |
|----|-----------------|----------|-------------|--------|----------------|-------|---------|
| R-001 | Supplier capacity constraint | Supply Chain | High | High | Qualify second source | John D | Open |

**Risk Categories:**
- Technical/Engineering
- Supply Chain
- Manufacturing/Quality
- Schedule
- Resource/Staffing
- External Dependencies

**Severity Matrix:**
- **Probability**: Low / Medium / High
- **Impact**: Low / Medium / High / Critical
- **Priority**: Risk score (P x I)

## Best Practices

### Communication Principles

1. **Be Clear and Concise**: Executives have limited time
2. **Lead with the Bottom Line**: Put the key message first
3. **Use Data**: Quantify impact and progress
4. **Be Transparent**: Red status early is better than surprise slips
5. **Propose Solutions**: Don't just raise problems
6. **Follow Up**: Close the loop on action items

### Risk Management Principles

1. **Identify Early**: Surface risks as soon as possible
2. **Quantify Impact**: Always estimate schedule/cost impact
3. **Have Mitigation Plans**: Every risk needs an owner and plan
4. **Review Regularly**: Weekly risk reviews minimum
5. **Escalate Appropriately**: Don't sit on critical risks

### Program Management Principles

1. **Single Source of Truth**: One master schedule, one risk log
2. **Define DRIs Clearly**: Ambiguity kills programs
3. **Track Dependencies**: Map and monitor critical interfaces
4. **Regular Cadence**: Predictable meeting and reporting rhythms
5. **Data-Driven**: Use metrics to inform decisions

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Program Kickoff
"I'm starting a new hardware program. Help me create a program charter and initial RACI matrix."

**Output**: Complete program charter with objectives, scope, stakeholders, RACI matrix, and initial risk register.

### Scenario 2: Weekly Status Update
"Generate my weekly status update for the executive team. Here's what happened this week: [details]"

**Output**: Formatted status update with accomplishments, risks, decisions needed, and next steps.

### Scenario 3: Factory Issue
"Our factory is reporting yield issues on component X. Help me draft a communication."

**Output**: Professional, action-oriented email requesting specific information and proposing next steps.

### Scenario 4: Escalation Preparation
"I need to escalate a supplier delay to my VP. The issue is [details]. Help me prepare the escalation."

**Output**: Structured escalation narrative with situation, impact, options, and recommendation.
